A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

Structure and honest communication are the two things that mainly come to mind when I think about this. With self guided learning being such a large part of this experience self imposed structure is a key pillar to success in this for me. Being able to plan ahead, know what I am supposed to be doing and when to be doing it removes a lot of the anxiety from the experience and allows me to just dive into the work when I need/want to & also not feel guilty when not working. That paired with honest communication allows me to stay accountable to myself & others during this course, letting others to be able to rely on me when needed and not let myself down with my own predetermined goals. Lastly, what will I do when it all gets a bit much and I can feel the pressure building? Firstly knowing that I am not alone in this experience is reassuring & the support structure available makes me feel that even if something is getting to me there is help just a message away. This makes the whole experience feel less isolating & that in itself helps ease a lot to begin with. Aside from that I am aware of my own emotions and any affect that might be having is the other half to this. I might just need to get some fresh air, take a walk or chat to someone else, whatever it might be as long as one of these actions is taken so not to let in boil up inside you and become worse than it needs to be.

Scheduling information?

Working from home it does feel a little cabin fevery sometimes which is why most mornings I aim to do something that gets me out of the house whether it’s going to gym, running errands or getting some snacks for the day ahead I have found this gets that distraction out of the way for me and allows me settle into the work day better. Whatever it might be I always aim to have my work day started by 11am at the latest. With the expectation of my weekly catch up on thursday mornings of course.
